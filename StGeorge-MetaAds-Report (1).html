TWKY — Strategy Recommendations

Where we’d take the account next

Prepared by TWKY. The sections in the other two views review what the St George Meta account has done. This view is our forward-looking recommendation set — the structural, creative, audience and targeting changes we would prioritise to build on that performance.

RECOMMENDATION 01

Irrelevant creative asset in the Pre-KW campaign

An Early Childhood Educator image has been appearing in the Pre-KW Campaign. This is an irrelevant creative that does not align with the campaign’s intended audience or messaging.

Action required. Turn off “Relevant Media” in the campaign’s ad settings to prevent Meta from automatically pulling unrelated assets into placements.

RECOMMENDATION 02

Campaign structure: separate physical & online batches

Physical and Online batch campaigns should be separated into distinct campaigns. The audiences for these two delivery modes differ geographically – physical batch prospects are concentrated in specific locations, while online batch prospects can be sourced nationwide or beyond.

Running them together forces a single targeting and bidding strategy on two fundamentally different audience pools, which limits Meta’s ability to optimise delivery for either.

RECOMMENDATION 03

Ad-level creative enhancements

Turn off automatic creative features from the Ad Settings. Select only the relevant enhancements at the ad level if necessary. Otherwise, certain creative enhancements (such as text overlays, cropping adjustments, and music additions) will be automatically enabled by Meta, potentially distorting the intended creative execution.

RECOMMENDATION 04

Custom audience strategy

Implement custom audiences to leverage existing data assets and improve targeting precision:

  • Students as existing customers – Upload current and past student lists to create a customer audience for exclusion or lookalike modelling.
  • All leads as engaged customers – Upload the full lead database to build an engaged audience segment for retargeting and lookalike expansion.
  • Website visitors as a data source – Even though the pixel is not currently active, the website can still be used as a data source to identify traffic and build potential customer audiences. Activating the Meta Pixel should be treated as a priority action item.
RECOMMENDATION 05

Creative strategy overhaul

The current creatives follow the same theme and template. This uniform approach limits Meta’s machine learning from effectively identifying new audiences and prospects, as there is insufficient creative variation for the algorithm to test and optimise against.

The creative strategy needs to be restructured to cover the full marketing funnel – Top of Funnel (awareness), Middle of Funnel (consideration), and Bottom of Funnel (conversion) – with distinct content types at each stage.

Recommended content types
  • Founder stories – Build brand credibility and emotional connection (Top of Funnel)
  • Lecturer recommendations – Showcase academic expertise and quality (Middle of Funnel)
  • Past student testimonials – Provide social proof and trust signals (Middle/Bottom of Funnel)
  • Current student activities – Highlight programme-related experiences and campus life (Top/Middle of Funnel)

All of the above content types should be produced and launched as video assets for maximum engagement and algorithmic preference.

Creative format & placement specifications

Ads must also be launched with image assets optimised for different placements:

  • 1080 × 1350 px – Feed placements (Instagram & Facebook)
  • 1080 × 1920 px – Stories & Reels placements

Additionally, animated videos with detailed programme information are recommended to convey key selling points in an engaging, scroll-stopping format.

RECOMMENDATION 06

Targeting strategy

The targeting strategy should be aligned with each programme’s delivery mode:

Geo-targeted audiences for physical batches – Use location-based targeting to reach prospects within commutable distance of the physical campus or learning centre.

Broad targeting for online batches – Remove geographic restrictions to maximise reach for programmes delivered entirely online.

As an additional test, consider removing detailed interest-based targeting entirely and increasing the number of ad creatives within each ad set. This forces Meta’s algorithm to learn and identify audiences through creative performance signals rather than relying on predefined interest categories – often yielding more efficient results at scale.
